Research

I am an experimental physicist broadly interested in precision measurements of the cosmic microwave background (CMB), line-intensity mapping at millimeter wavelenghts, and direct dark matter detection with new technologies. I am involved with the SPT-3G project, which is currently performing a CMB survey with the South Pole Telescope (SPT). Upcoming cameras in development include: SPT-SLIM, a camera on the SPT consisting of on-chip kinetic inductance detector (KID) spectrometers to perform LIM and millimeter wavelenghts; SPT-3G+, a proposed high-frequency CMB camera using KIDs; and CMB-S4, the ultimate ground-based CMB experiment to search for inflationary B-mode polarization with the CMB.

SPT-3G

The SPT-3G experiment is performing a multi-year survey with 15,000 polarization-sensitive detectors to map the CMB and constrain inflation, dark energy, and neutrino properties.

CMB-S4

In the late 2020s, CMB-S4 will deploy 500,000 detectors across a dozen telescopes at South Pole and in the Atacama desert of Chile. It will provide the definitive ground-based constraints on inflation and neutrino properties using the CMB. Fermilab is leading the design, integration, and testing of cryogenic modules of detectors and readout electronics in the CMB cameras.

Readout for Superconducting Resonators

State-of-the-art RFSoC FPGAs from Xilinx offer the possibility of a new generation of simple, low-cost readout electronics for microwave kinetics inductance detectors (KIDs). We are working with engineers at Fermilab, led by Gustavo Cancelo, to develop and test firmware for KID readouts based on this architecture.

Past Experiments

In a long-lost life, I worked on direct detection of particle dark matter using the SuperCDMS experiment and x-ray detectors. It has been a while since I seriously worked in this field, but I maintain a strong latent interest in dark matter and love talking to colleagues at the forefront.

Education and Outreach

I have been involved in a range of education and public outreach activities. Several long-duration efforts are highlighted below.

Conquering the Physics GRE

As grad students at MIT, Yoni Kahn and I were troubled by the lack of study material for the physics GRE, so in a burst of youthful indiscretion we decided to write our own. A few editions and publishers in, the book seems to have proven useful for many students applying to grad school. Check it out at your favorite bookseller.

Saturday Morning Physics

Started by Nobel laureate Leon Lederman in 1980, Saturday Morning Physics has an illustrious history at Fermilab. High school students come to the lab for eleven Saturdays, listen to lectures on Fermilab science, and take tours of the laboratory. Bryan Ramson and I are currently serving as co-directors of the program together. Together we are guiding the program through the COVID era and working to make the program available to a more diverse population in the city of Chicago.
